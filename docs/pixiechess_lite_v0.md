# PixieChess-Lite v0

PixieChess-Lite v0 is the research ruleset used for scaled proof-of-concept
training. It is intentionally not a full claim of FIDE chess fidelity.

## Objective

The game objective is to remove the opposing king from the board. A side wins
when its king is active and the opponent's king is inactive. If both kings are
inactive, the game is a draw.

## Simulator Contract

- Legal moves are generated by the deterministic simulator.
- The model never decides legality or next-state transitions.
- Each training start state must contain exactly one active king per side.
- Active pieces may not share squares.
- Every active piece instance must reference a known piece class.
- Hook/event cascades must resolve deterministically or fail loudly at the
  cascade safety limit.
- Replay traces must round-trip to the same final state hash.

## Deliberate Non-Goals

The proof does not depend on exact handling of castling, en passant,
fifty-move draws, or other orthodox chess edge cases. Those mechanics may exist
in the implementation, but the scaling claim is about learning strategic play
in a deterministic chess-like game with arbitrary executable magical pieces.

## Promotion Evidence

A checkpoint is considered stronger only through arena evidence against a
previous champion or a search-only baseline. Training and validation loss are
health metrics, not promotion criteria.
